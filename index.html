Rules Summary

  • Player 1 sets up all pieces first, north of centerline.
  • Player 2 sets up pieces south of centerline.
  • Player 1 takes the first turn.
  • A turn consists of up to two moves of the player's pieces and one push.
  • The push is mandatory; if you cannot sucessfully push you lose.
  • A piece can be moved to any other it can reach via unoccupied squares.
  • To push: the current player selects one of his or her square pieces and a direction. The push must be in the direction of an adjacent piece. All pieces in that direction are pushed one square, provided that the push is not blocked by a wall or an anchored piece.
  • Once a player successfully pushes, the turn ends and the active player switches.
  • Play continues until a player's piece is pushed from the board or a player cannot push when required. In either case, the other player is the winner.

How to Play

Pushfight is a game for two players. The goal of the game is to push one of your opponent's pieces off the board, or to prevent your opponent from having a legal push at the end of his or her turn.

Setup

The first player starts by placing its three square pieces and two round pieces anywhere on the top half of the board. The second player then responds, placing all five of its pieces on the bottom half of the board.

Movement and Pushes

Player 1 takes the first turn. A turn consists of up to two moves followed by a push. A player may move any of its pieces to any square on the board it can reach by empty spaces. Pieces cannot move through occupied squares or off the board.

After finishing movement, the active player must make a push. To push, the active player chooses one of its square pieces. It then pushes an adjacent piece (up, down, left, or right) one square in that direction. Unless obstructed by a wall or anchored piece (see below), all pieces in a line from the pushing piece are moved one square in the direction of the push. If any piece leaves the board, the owner of that piece loses and the other player wins. After completing a push, the player's turn ends and the opponent's turn begins. Play continues until one player wins.

A push must:

  • Start with one of the active player's square pieces;
  • Move at least one additional piece;
  • Not be prevented by a wall or anchored piece.

A push may:

  • Be in any direction, provided at least one other piece is in that direction;
  • Push pieces of either color;
  • Cause the pushing player to lose the game by pushing his or her own piece from the board.

Anchored Pieces

After a player pushes a piece, that piece becomes anchored until the start of owner's next turn. This is the only piece that cannot be pushed. There will never be more than one anchored piece on the board at any time.
